# Eureka Ring Landing Page Content Brief

> Working slogan: **Forget nothing. Type nothing.**  
> Supporting line: **Tap. Talk. Done.**

This document describes the current landing-page content direction for **Eureka Ring**: a personal AI capture ring for recording anything worth keeping, controlling everyday software/devices, and giving quiet haptic feedback.

## 1. Positioning

**Eureka Ring is not a health tracker.**  
It is a personal AI capture and control device worn on the finger.

The core promise:

> Say something once, and Eureka turns it into the right thing: a note, task, expense, reminder, score, list, or log. Then use gestures to control the tools around you.

### What It Is

- A personal recording and capture tool.
- A quick-input layer for ideas, tasks, expenses, reminders, sports logs, daily notes, and other small records.
- A gesture controller for music, desktop workflows, and basic device control.
- A haptic, screenless wearable that confirms actions privately.

### What It Is Not

- Not a health ring.
- Not a meeting recorder.
- Not an always-on surveillance device.
- Not a generic smart jewelry product.
- Not a developer-only gadget, even though it can control desktop workflows.

## 2. Audience

Primary audience:

- People who capture many small things during the day but hate opening apps.
- AI tool users who want faster input without typing.
- Founders, builders, creators, students, athletes, parents, and people managing personal tasks.
- People who want a low-friction personal assistant without wearing a camera or pendant.

Secondary audience:

- Productivity enthusiasts.
- Early adopters of AI hardware.
- People interested in wearable controllers.

## 3. Core Narrative

The page should build around three pillars:

### 1. Capture Anything

The ring captures short, intentional voice notes and sends them into UReka, where AI sorts them into the right destination.

Examples:

- “Spent $14 on lunch.” → Expense
- “Add oat milk to the shopping list.” → Shopping list
- “Won the first set 6–4.” → Match log
- “Remind me to call mom at 6.” → Reminder
- “Idea: a ring that captures everything.” → Note
- “Practice serve for 20 minutes tomorrow.” → Task / training log

The point is not “capture thoughts” only. The broader claim is:

> If it can be recorded, Eureka Ring can capture it.

### 2. Control Your World

Touch gestures turn the ring into a lightweight controller.

Examples:

- Play / pause music.
- Previous / next track.
- Scroll conversation history.
- Submit voice input on desktop.
- Clear and re-record if the input was wrong.
- Trigger software actions through configured gestures.

The desktop version can be stronger and app-specific. Mobile should stay lighter and more publishable: basic media control and UReka capture.

### 3. Feel the Response

The built-in haptic motor confirms actions without a screen.

Examples:

- One vibration: capture started.
- Two vibrations: capture saved.
- Subtle nudge: reminder or next action.
- Silent confirmation while in public.

This makes the ring feel like a two-way assistant, not just a passive microphone.

Archived alternative:

### Main Motion Concept

The page uses one large ring as a persistent visual thread:

- Hero: the ring enters from the bottom, leaving the headline unobstructed.
- Capture / Control / Feel: the ring rolls between sections.
- Proof sections: the ring fades into a supporting watermark, allowing content proof to lead.
- Reserve: the ring becomes a stable product anchor again.

This motion works because a ring is circular and symmetrical. It can roll convincingly using 2D rotation before final product renders are ready.

## 7. Tone and Copy Rules

Use:

- Clear, confident, simple English.
- Short sentences.
- Product proof over abstract AI language.
- “Capture”, “control”, “feel”, “personal”, “private”.

Avoid:

- Over-indexing on “thoughts”. It can capture more than thoughts.
- Health-tracking language.
- Meeting-recorder claims.
- Surveillance-like language.
- Supplier names or chip model numbers.
- Over-promising arbitrary phone app control.
